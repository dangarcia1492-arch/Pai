United Kingdom

No standalone AI statute. The UK governs artificial intelligence through existing sectoral regimes — data protection, financial conduct, online safety and cyber — with the DPDI Act now rolling into force and an AI Cyber Code of Practice out for public consultation.

01 — In force

What's binding today

Four instruments do most of the work. There is no UK AI Act — obligations attach through data protection, online safety, competition and financial-conduct frameworks that already apply to AI systems.

In force
Data Protection and Digital Information Act 2024
Amends UK GDPR and the Data Protection Act 2018. Rolling commencement through 2026; ICO has issued guidance on DSARs under the new regime.
ICO Assented Jul 2024
In force
Online Safety Act 2023
Duty-of-care regime for user-to-user and search services, including AI-generated content. Codes of Practice on illegal-content duties now in force; children's safety codes follow.
OFCOM Phased, from 2025
In force
Digital Markets, Competition and Consumers Act 2024
CMA's Digital Markets Unit can now designate firms with Strategic Market Status and impose conduct requirements — the primary lever for foundation-model competition oversight.
CMA Effective 2025
In force
Financial sector supervisory expectations
FCA Consumer Duty plus PRA SS1/23 (model risk management) extend to AI systems in consumer and prudential supervision. Operational-resilience rules apply to third-party AI providers.
FCA · PRA Active supervision
02 — On the table

What's being decided

Three live processes worth watching — one statutory consultation with a hard deadline, two rolling requests for comment that will shape how frontier AI and operational resilience are supervised.

Open
AI Cyber Security Code of Practice
DSIT voluntary code becoming the baseline reference for AI-system security across government procurement. Public consultation on scope and certification model.
DSIT Closes 12 May
Open
Third-party evaluations framework
AISI request for comment on how independent evaluators will be credentialed to test frontier models — the UK's counterpart to the EU AI Act's notified-body regime.
AISI Rolling, no deadline
Open
Operational resilience — critical third parties
PRA consultation on how systemic technology providers (including frontier-AI suppliers) will be brought inside the financial-services resilience perimeter.
PRA · BoE Closes 04 Jun
03 — Who regulates

The anchor set

Six bodies most legal and compliance desks watch for the UK. Thirty-five more — including the Ada Lovelace Institute, the Investigatory Powers Tribunal, and departmental select committees — are behind the paywall.

Data protection authority
Information Commissioner's Office
ICO · Wilmslow
Independent regulator for data protection and information rights; issues binding guidance on AI transparency, automated decision-making and DSARs.
Cyber agency
National Cyber Security Centre
NCSC · London
Technical authority for UK cyber security; publishes frontier-AI cyber advisories and supplier-vetting guidance used across government procurement.
Financial regulator
Financial Conduct Authority
FCA · London
Conduct supervisor for UK financial services; applies the Consumer Duty and algorithmic-fairness expectations to consumer-facing AI in banking, insurance and investments.
Competition authority
Competition and Markets Authority
CMA · London
Foundation-model competition lead; operates the Strategic Market Status regime under the DMCC Act and publishes the AI foundation-models programme.
Telecoms regulator
Office of Communications
OFCOM · London
Sole enforcer of the Online Safety Act 2023; sets and polices codes of practice covering illegal content, children's safety and AI-generated material.
Government agency
AI Safety Institute
AISI · London
Frontier-model evaluations and red-teaming; produces the UK's technical evidence base on advanced-AI risk and informs international safety commitments.
