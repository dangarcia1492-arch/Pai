Meta Platforms Ireland fined €310m in cross-border ad-targeting decision

Ireland's Data Protection Commission has concluded its Article 60 inquiry into Meta Platforms Ireland's behavioural advertising operations, imposing an administrative fine of €310m and ordering the company to bring processing into compliance within six months.

Administrative fine
€310,000,000
Six-month compliance window. EDPB binding decision underpins the lawful-basis finding.

The decision, delivered this morning, concludes a cross-border inquiry under Article 60 GDPR in which the Irish DPC acted as Lead Supervisory Authority. Concerned supervisory authorities in Spain (AEPD), the Netherlands (AP) and Italy (Garante) formally disagreed with the DPC's draft decision, triggering resolution by the European Data Protection Board under Article 65. The EDPB's binding decision now underpins the final finding on lawful basis.

Meta Platforms Ireland had argued that behavioural advertising processing could be justified under Article 6(1)(f) GDPR — legitimate interests — when supported by appropriate user controls and transparency. The DPC, applying the EDPB's Article 65 decision, rejected that analysis on the basis of both the scale of the processing and the reasonable expectations of data subjects who primarily engage with Meta's services for social and messaging purposes.

Key findings

The decision sets out four substantive findings, each of which has practical consequences beyond Meta itself.

First, the decision confirms that the scale of processing is a material factor in the legitimate-interests balancing test. The EDPB explicitly states that processing of the volume and scope described "cannot reasonably be characterised as what a typical user would expect" when the primary purpose of the service is social communication rather than advertising. This extends Article 6(1)(f) analysis in a way that will affect every large-scale consumer platform's position paper.
