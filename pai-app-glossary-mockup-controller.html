Rev 2 compare notes

  • Variant 1 (table) — strongest at-a-glance. Strips down clean once the gloss/test/verify-chips are gone. Now just term · article · verbatim · source per column.
  • Variant 2 (accordion) — kept but acknowledged as "scannable yet fiddly" per Dave's read. Cleaner without flags + draft chips, but the click-to-reveal pattern still buries the verbatim.
  • Variant 3 (canonical + cognates + switcher) — adds the canonical-switcher you raised; source links now on every cognate; flags gone. Best balance of hierarchy + flexibility.
  • Variant 4 (term-pivot tabs) — new shape replacing the equal-weight stack. Treats all 5 regimes as peers; one is "in focus" at a time. "Compare all 5 side-by-side →" can deep-link back to Variant 1's table for direct comparison.
  • Variant 5 (anchor-on-two) — new this rev, from Dave's prompt. V3's right-hand side gets promoted to a full anchor; both regimes get equal real estate and full verbatim. Swap-right control sits in a strip above so options don't crowd the comparison. Best when the reader is doing a deliberate bilateral compare (e.g. "GDPR vs DPDPA right now").
