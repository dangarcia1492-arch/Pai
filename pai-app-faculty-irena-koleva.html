Biography

Practice and focus

Irena Koleva is the contributor of the Bulgaria AI Note for PrinciplesAI. Her analysis covers the operation of the EU AI Act in Bulgaria, the pending national Bill on the Use and Development of Artificial Intelligence, GDPR-anchored automated decision-making, and the points of legal friction surfacing in Bulgarian courts.

The Bulgaria AI Note tracks the Council of Ministers Decision No 398 of 18 June 2025 designating fundamental-rights authorities under Article 77 of the EU AI Act, the two Bulgarian preliminary-ruling references to the CJEU (Case C-806/24 from the Sofia District Court on automated telecom billing, and Case C-245/25 on AI in expert reports), and the parallel work of the Ministry of Electronic Governance on Article 28/70 designations.

Bulgaria AI Note coverage

Six sections, EU-anchored, friction-aware

The Bulgaria AI Note is structured as Part B narrative (six sections) sitting alongside Part A matrix data on the Bulgaria jurisdiction page.

B1
Legal Characterisation of AI
No dedicated AI law in force; EU AI Act applies directly. National AI Bill tabled 8 October 2025 to complement the EU framework.
B2
Relationship to Higher-Level Law
EU AI Act as primary regime; national AI Bill adds stricter bans (remote biometric ID, AI in judiciary, deepfake/political-ad rules) and sectoral amendments.
B3
Legal Bases Relied on in Practice
GDPR + Bulgarian PDPA, equality and fundamental-rights provisions, consumer-protection and product-safety law, sectoral regulators. CJEU references C-806/24 and C-245/25 illustrate emerging Bulgarian AI litigation pathways.
B4
Data, Inference and Automated Decision-Making
GDPR Article 22 anchors heightened scrutiny of solely-automated decisions with legal or similarly significant effects.
B5
Points of Legal Friction
Tension between AI training and GDPR purpose-limitation; copyright and authorship for generative outputs; AI Bill provisions exceeding the EU AI Act baseline.
B6
Legislative Developments
CMD 398/2025 fundamental-rights designations · pending AI Bill · draft Council of Ministers decisions for Article 28/70 notifying authorities · Strategy for Digital Transformation 2026–2030.
