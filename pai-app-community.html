GPAI downstream duties: how are deployers splitting the Annex XII work from provider onboarding?

14 replies6 participantsstarted 2h ago
HK
Helena Kowalski
Head of Privacy, Lindqvist & Bauer
2h ago

We are mid-reorg. The new RFP template is trying to cover both provider attestations and the deployer-side risk register in one document. I suspect this is a mistake, but the procurement team likes the single-artefact view.

Two specific questions. First, when a provider already supplies an Annex XII disclosure, how much of it are you treating as deployer input versus independent review? Second, for multi-model integrations, does anyone maintain separate registers per model, or one consolidated register with a provider matrix?

SL
Sophie Laurent
DPO, Banque Atlantique
1h ago

We split the two. Provider disclosure goes into an attestations register owned by procurement; the deployer risk register sits with the second line. Pains at first, worth it when a provider switches mid-contract and nothing has to be renamed.

For multi-model, we run one consolidated register with a provider matrix column. Copying per-model was unmanageable past four models.

JR
Jonas Reinhardt
GC, Reinhardt Industrie
38m ago

Agree with Sophie on the split. One warning: if you run a consolidated register, be very careful that your provider-specific controls do not get averaged out. The notified body pushed back hard when ours did.

First, when a provider already supplies an Annex XII disclosure, how much of it are you treating as deployer input versus independent review?

We treat it as input. Not review. The independent-review position got us into an awkward conversation about who has the technical competence.

PS
Priya Sundaram
Compliance Director, Mercer Halliday
24m ago

Single-artefact view never survives audit. Every time we have tried it the audit team has forced us back to two artefacts with a mapping table.

DL
David Longford
Founder, Principles AI
11m ago

Noting that the AI Office draft guidance on Annex XII (26 March) explicitly contemplates the deployer-side artefact as distinct. I will post the relevant paragraphs as a separate thread later today for reference.
