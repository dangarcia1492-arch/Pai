United Kingdom

No dedicated AI Act. The UK governs artificial intelligence through existing sectoral law — data protection, financial conduct, cybersecurity — coordinated by regulators who publish their own guidance rather than a unified statute.

What applies today
Data protection
UK GDPR & Data Protection Act
Covers automated decision-making, profiling, and transparency obligations for AI systems handling personal data. DPDI amendments rolling out this spring add explicit rules for AI training data and subject-access rights over model outputs.
Live
Financial services
FCA & PRA supervisory expectations
Consumer Duty and SS1/23 model-risk expectations apply to AI systems in scope of financial regulation. Sector-specific but enforced with teeth — algorithmic lending, trading, and advice are already under supervisory notice activity.
Live
Online content
Online Safety Act 2023
Ofcom-enforced duties on user-to-user and search services, with direct relevance to generative-AI products that produce or host third-party content.
