Sweden Enforcement Integritetsskyddsmyndigheten (IMY) 28 Jan 2026
IMY fines Sportadmin SEK 6 million for unlawful processing of 2.1 million individuals' personal data
Sweden's data protection authority has issued a SEK 6 million administrative fine against Sportadmin AB following an investigation into the company's processing of personal data belonging to approximately 2.1 million individuals associated with Swedish sports organisations.

IMY determined that Sportadmin AB processed personal data without a valid legal basis under Article 6 GDPR and failed to implement adequate technical and organisational security measures as required by Article 32 GDPR. The investigation found that personal data including names, contact details and membership information had been retained beyond the periods permissible under applicable retention rules.

IMY further found that Sportadmin failed to provide adequate information to data subjects in accordance with Articles 13 and 14 GDPR, and that the company's data processing agreements with sports organisations lacked the specificity required under Article 28 GDPR. The authority ordered Sportadmin to bring its processing into compliance within six months.

India Guidance IRDAI 6 Apr 2026
IRDAI issues revised information and cyber security guidelines for India's insurance sector
India's insurance sector regulator has issued revised information and cyber security guidelines requiring all insurers, insurance intermediaries and the Insurance Information Bureau of India to implement updated minimum standards and governance mechanisms with effect from the current financial year.

IRDAI issued Circular IRDAI/GA&HR/CIR/MISC/51/4/2026 on 6 April 2026. The revised guidelines supersede the 2023 circular and reflect the evolving cyber threat landscape, feedback received from the industry, and recommendations of IRDAI committees. All regulated entities are required to comply from financial year 2026–27.

United Kingdom Regulatory Financial Conduct Authority 21 Apr 2026
FCA selects eight firms for second cohort of AI Live Testing programme
On 21 April 2026, the Financial Conduct Authority announced the selection of eight firms for the second cohort of its AI Live Testing initiative. The FCA states the programme enables safe and responsible deployment of artificial intelligence applications in UK financial services by providing a structured supervisory environment. Selected firms will work directly with FCA supervisors to test AI use cases including fraud detection, credit decisioning and client communication tools before wider deployment.
European Union Opinion European Data Protection Board 27 Apr 2026
EDPB issues opinion on SLB Group controller binding corporate rules approval
On 15 April 2026, the EDPB adopted Opinion 10/2026 on the draft decision of the Dutch Supervisory Authority regarding the approval of Controller Binding Corporate Rules for the SLB Group, exercising its role under Article 64(1)(f) GDPR to ensure consistent application of international data transfer safeguards. The EDPB states the BCRs must clarify the scope of data categories transferred and strengthen provisions relating to data subject rights enforcement in third countries.
Germany Guidance Niedersachsen LfD 27 Apr 2026
Niedersachsen data protection authority publishes AI Act guidance for professionals
On 27 April 2026, the Niedersächsischer Landesbeauftragte für den Datenschutz issued practical FAQs addressing legal and data protection requirements for artificial intelligence system use under the EU AI Act. The authority recognises that many organisations face uncertainty regarding the intersection of AI Act obligations and GDPR requirements, particularly for AI systems that process personal data. The guidance addresses risk classification, transparency obligations and the delineation of AI provider and deployer responsibilities.
France Guidance CNIL 27 Apr 2026
CNIL recommends data protection officer activity reports as compliance management tool
On 27 April 2026, the CNIL published guidance recommending that data protection officers prepare annual activity reports to document compliance efforts and report to senior management. The CNIL notes that such reports are not legally mandated under the GDPR but are described as good practice that enables organisations to demonstrate accountability under Article 5(2) GDPR. The guidance sets out a suggested structure covering the DPO's advisory activities, data breach management and staff training outcomes.
Czech Republic Report NÚKIB 22 Apr 2026
Czech cyber authority reports 70 incidents in Q1 2026, highest quarterly figure on record
On 22 April 2026, the Czech National Cyber and Information Security Office published its quarterly threat overview for Q1 2026, documenting over 70 cybersecurity incidents, the highest number recorded in the preceding twelve months. NÚKIB identified a marked increase in cyber incidents driven by state-sponsored actors targeting critical infrastructure and public sector entities. The authority states that ransomware attacks against healthcare providers and DDoS campaigns against financial institutions accounted for the largest share of incidents.
Australia Inter-agency OAIC · eSafety Commissioner 23 Apr 2026
OAIC and eSafety Commissioner establish formal cooperation agreement on privacy and online safety
On 23 April 2026, the Office of the Australian Information Commissioner and the eSafety Commissioner entered into a Memorandum of Understanding to coordinate regulatory responses where privacy and online safety intersect. The OAIC states the MoU formalises existing collaborative efforts and establishes formal communication channels for sharing intelligence on emerging harms and coordinating enforcement action where both privacy and online safety considerations arise in the same matter.
Canada Parliamentary submission Office of the Privacy Commissioner 22 Apr 2026
OPC supports Bill S-5 digital health interoperability with privacy conditions
On 22 April 2026, Deputy Commissioner Marc Chénier of the Office of the Privacy Commissioner of Canada delivered a statement to the Senate Standing Committee on Social Affairs, Science and Technology endorsing Bill S-5, the Connected Care for Canadians Act, conditional on adequate privacy protections being embedded in accompanying regulations. The OPC states that the bill's health data interoperability framework must be accompanied by binding rules on purpose limitation, patient consent and cross-border transfer controls to comply with applicable federal privacy obligations.
