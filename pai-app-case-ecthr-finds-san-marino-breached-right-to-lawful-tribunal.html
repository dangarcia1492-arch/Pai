Judgment

On 9 April 2026, the European Court of Human Rights (Fifth Section) delivered its judgment in Simoncini v. San Marino (Application no. 3106/24), finding a violation of Article 6 § 1 of the Convention.

The case concerned disciplinary proceedings against Massimiliano Simoncini, a member of the San Marino judiciary, for misconduct committed in February 2023. The Judicial Council (Consiglio Giudiziario) issued him a warning (ammonimento) on 13 July 2023.

Simoncini appealed to the Constitutional Court (Collegio garante della costituzionalità delle norme) on 4 September 2023. The President of the Constitutional Court declared the appeal inadmissible by decrees of 12 September 2023 and 22 September 2023, citing the applicant's initial lack of legal representation.

The Court found that the inadmissibility decrees were issued by the President of the Constitutional Court sitting alone, rather than by a panel as required under domestic law. It held this breached the applicant's right to a tribunal established by law. The Court rejected the Government's preliminary objection that the application constituted an abuse of the right of individual application under Article 35 § 3 (a) of the Convention.
